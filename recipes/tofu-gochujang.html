Oven-cooked gochujang tofu

Description

This is not a proper recipe, it is just a good thing to do with tofu. Personally, I hate cooking tofu in a pan, so I decided to stop and do it in the oven instead.

I have not tried this. I love tofu, but usually just "blag" it. This is not precise. Just go with your heart!

Ingredients

Totally made up proportions. I also never bother to squeeze my tofu properly and it still tastes good.

  • big block of plain/smoked firm tofu you can get.
  • 1 tbsp of Gochujang.
  • salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp of sesame oil
  • ¾ tbsp of white wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp of cornstarch

Steps

  1. Drain the tofu.
  2. Cut up the tofu into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Squeeze (e.g., put onto wooden/bamboo chopping board, cover with kitchen towel or tea towel, and put something heavy on top).
  4. Meanwhile, pre-heat oven to 180 °C. You can return to recipe once it is nearly pre-heated.
  5. Mix the oil, vinegar, and gochujang in a bowl. If it's super thick, add some soy sauce or hot water to thin it.
  6. Pour this mix over the tofu.
  7. Mix well - ensure the tofu is covered in sauce. Do not worry if it gets a bit broken up.
  8. Add cornstarch and mix in. Add salt and pepper. Mix until combined.
  9. Transfer to baking tray, evenly spaced. I put it on greaseproof baking paper, but it'll probably be fine without.
  10. Bake for 30 minutes - taking them out and flipping them over every 10 minutes or so.

Alternatively, just buy an air fryer.

Apparently, you can store leftover baked tofu in a freezer-safe container and freeze for up to 6 months. However, I have never experienced lefover tofu. It always gets eaten within 2 days. Theoretically, if you freeze it, you would let it thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Healthy vegan flapjacks

Description

Ever feel hungry? Flapjacks can solve this issue! They can fill that gap between meals with their oaty goodness. If you add seeds instead of nuts, you (or your child) can take them into school! If this doesn't apply to you, then you can add nuts, yay!

This recipe is totally nicked from a blog. They adapted another flapjack recipe to be less chocolate-y. If you prefer chocolate, you can follow this link, then their link to the chocolate flapjack recipe.

Ingredients

I have changed some of their ingredients slightly, so if you want a tested recipe, go to their blog!

  • 115g coconut oil
  • 150g sugar (soft brown sugar or whatever sugar you can find)
  • 2 tbsp molasses
  • 150g mix of dried fruit, chopped nuts, and/or seeds
  • 225g porridge oats
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)

Steps

  1. Grease a baking tin (15cm x 20cm approx)
  2. Pre-heat the oven to 160 °C/150 °C Fan Oven or Gas 3.
  3. Melt the spread with the sugar and molasses (in a pan or in the microwave).
  4. Stir in the dried fruit/nuts/seeds and the vanilla extract. Into a bowl or the pan.
  5. Mix in the oats until coated in the syrup.
  6. Pour into the greased baking tin and flatten with a fork or spoon.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown.
  8. Cool in the tin for 5-10 minutes before marking into squares/bars with a knife.

Cut into flapjacks when cool - not before, or they'll fall apart! If this happens, do not despair, you have granola.

Suitable for freezing.

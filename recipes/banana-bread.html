Risky whisky banana bread

Description

I have a feeling that my daughter doubled a normal banana bread recipe and tried to fit it into a reasonably small baking tin, which resulted in the inside being uncooked. Therefore, maybe have several baking recepticles on hand to shove the mix into.

Also note that the original recipe did not have whisky in, I added it because it rhymed with "risky" and I do not advise you to follow this recipe.

Ingredients

  • 6 bananas 🍌
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup rapeseed oil (or some other cooking oil)
  • 4 tsp (cheap) whisky (or vanilla extract, rum etc.)
  • 4 cups plain flour
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 cups chopped nuts (or seeds)
  • more sugar to sprinkle on top at the end

Steps

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 175 °C.
  2. Mash bananas in a big bowl (this is where the rest of the stuff is going too).
  3. Add sugar, oil, and whisky/vanilla. Give a little mix.
  4. Sieve in flour and baking soda. Add cinnamon and salt.
  5. Stir everything together - do not overmix!
  6. Fold in the nuts/seeds.
  7. Sprinkle the sugar on top (up to half a cup).
  8. Bake for 30 - 50 minutes. Peek through door part-way through.
  9. Test it with a spike thing. If it looks cooked through, put it on top of a wire thing to cool properly.

I like to eat some while it is still warm! But I leave most of it to cool.

💀 Full disclosure: I have never tried whisky in a banana bread. If you genuinely want to risk trying this recipe with alcohol, maybe rum would be better.
